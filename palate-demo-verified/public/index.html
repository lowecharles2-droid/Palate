Transparent scoring

“Authentic” means fidelity to what the restaurant claims to be.

The score is a Palate editorial judgment, not an objective fact. It is kept separate from the local/independent score.

9–10

Specialist menu with strong cuisine heritage, technique, family recipes, or direct evidence from the restaurant.

7–8

Clear and consistent category identity, but modernized, fusion-based, or delivered through a larger chain.

5–6

Broad or heavily adapted menu where cuisine identity is present but not the main specialist focus.

Separate local score

A chain can score highly for cuisine fidelity while scoring low for local ownership. Burger King is the clearest example.

Data policy: Palate does not invent calorie, protein, allergy, or halal certifications. Dietary tags indicate menu availability found on official sources and must be confirmed directly with the restaurant.
